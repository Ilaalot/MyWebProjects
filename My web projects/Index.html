Part One

Emperor Akbar was in the habit of puzzling riddles and Puzzles to his counter. He often ask questions which were strange and witty. It took much wisdom to answer these questions.

Part Two

Once he asked a very strange question. The counters were dumb and fooled by this questions. Akbar glanced at his counters. As he looked, one by one the heads began to hang below in search for answer. The was at this moment Birbal entered the courtyard. Birbal who knew nature of the Emperor quickly grasped the situaion and asked, "May i know the question so that i can try and answer".

Part Three

Akbar said, "How man crows are there in this city?" without even a moment's thought, Birbal replied "There are fifty thousand five hundred and eighty nine crows, My Lord".

Part Four

Emperor Akbar in court room

"How can you be so sure?" said Akbar, Birbal said, "Make sure you then count, My lord. if you find more crows it means some ave come to visit there relatives here. if you find less number of crows it means some have gone to visit there relatives elsewhere". Akbar was pleased very much by Birbal's will.
